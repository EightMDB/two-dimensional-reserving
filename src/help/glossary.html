Actuarial Glossary

Terms, definitions, and formulas reference

Common Terms

Chain Ladder Method

A reserving technique that uses historical loss development patterns to project future claim development. Also known as the development method or link ratio method.

Development Factor (LDF)

The ratio of cumulative losses at one valuation point to cumulative losses at an earlier valuation point. Used to project ultimate losses.

LDF = Cumulative Losses(t+1) / Cumulative Losses(t)

IBNR (Incurred But Not Reported)

Reserves for losses that have occurred but have not yet been reported to the insurer. Often used to refer to all types of reserve development, including IBNER.

IBNR = Ultimate Losses - Paid Losses - Case Reserves

Ultimate Loss

The total estimated amount of all losses for a given accident period, including paid losses, case reserves, and IBNR.

Loss Ratio

The ratio of incurred losses to earned premium, typically expressed as a percentage. A key metric for evaluating underwriting profitability.

Loss Ratio = Incurred Losses / Earned Premium

Tail Factor

An additional development factor applied beyond the last observed development period to account for continued development to ultimate.

Accident Year

The calendar year in which a loss event occurred, regardless of when it was reported or paid.

Development Period

The time elapsed since the accident date, typically measured in months or years. Used to track loss development patterns.

Bornhuetter-Ferguson Method

A reserving method that combines an a priori loss ratio estimate with observed loss development. Provides more stability than Chain Ladder for immature years.

IBNR = (Expected Losses × % Unreported) + (Actual Losses - Expected Losses × % Reported)

Cape Cod Method

A variant of Bornhuetter-Ferguson that estimates the expected loss ratio from the data itself, rather than requiring an external a priori estimate.

Benktander Method

An iterative method that combines Chain Ladder and Bornhuetter-Ferguson, typically converging after 1-2 iterations to produce a credibility-weighted estimate.

Triangle

A matrix displaying loss data by accident year and development period, forming a triangular shape as older years have more development periods observed.

Cumulative Paid

The total amount of claims paid from the beginning of an accident period through a given evaluation date.

Incremental Paid

The amount of claims paid during a specific development period, calculated as the difference between cumulative paid amounts.

Key Formulas

Age-to-Age Factor

f(n,n+1) = C(n+1) / C(n)

Where C(n) is cumulative paid at age n

Age-to-Ultimate Factor

F(n,∞) = f(n,n+1) × f(n+1,n+2) × ... × Tail

Product of all subsequent age-to-age factors

Ultimate Losses (CL)

Ultimate = C(current) × F(current,∞)

Current cumulative multiplied by age-to-ultimate

IBNR Reserve

IBNR = Ultimate - Cumulative Paid

Total reserve requirement
